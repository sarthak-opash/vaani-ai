MOTOR INSURANCE OVERVIEW:
eSanad offers a comprehensive AI-powered motor insurance platform allowing comparison of plans from 35+ top UAE insurance companies. Policies are fully RTA-compliant, offer paperless policy issuance, and come with a + AED 50 Gift Voucher (Amazon, Noon, Carrefour, etc.) instantly upon issuance.

TYPES OF MOTOR INSURANCE & PRICING:

Indicative Annual Premium Snapshot (TPL vs. Comprehensive):
- Sedan Cars: TPL starts from 622 AED | Comprehensive starts from 1,282 AED
- 4WD / SUVs: TPL starts from 822 AED | Comprehensive starts from 1,600 AED
- Chinese Cars: TPL starts from 622 AED | Comprehensive starts from 1,700 AED
- Pickup Trucks: TPL starts from 1,000 AED | Comprehensive starts from 1,800 AED
*Note: Actual premiums vary based on vehicle details, driver profile, and driving history.

A. COMPREHENSIVE MOTOR INSURANCE
Covers both own damage and third-party liabilities. Ideal for new, high-value, or financed cars.
- Own Damage Cover: Accidental damage, fire, theft, vandalism, and natural disasters (storms, floods).
- Third-Party Liability: Unlimited coverage for third-party bodily injury/death, and up to AED 1,000,000 for third-party property damage.
- Standard Inclusions: Windscreen/glass coverage, towing, and emergency medical expenses.
- Optional Add-ons: Agency repairs, zero depreciation, 24/7 roadside assistance, replacement car option, GCC coverage extension, and personal accident benefits.

B. THIRD-PARTY LIABILITY (TPL) INSURANCE
Mandatory minimum coverage required by UAE law to drive.
- Covers bodily injury/death to third parties (unlimited).
- Covers third-party property damage up to AED 1,000,000.
- Does NOT cover own vehicle damage, fire, theft, or personal accidents for the driver.

MOTOR INSURANCE FAQ:

Q: Is motor insurance mandatory in the UAE?
A: Yes, by UAE traffic laws, having at least valid Third-Party Liability (TPL) insurance is mandatory to register, renew, and drive any vehicle on UAE roads.

Q: Do you cover Electric Vehicles (EVs) and Chinese cars?
A: Yes, we provide specialized EV plans covering battery systems, charging cables, and power surges. We also offer dedicated Chinese Car Insurance for Geely, MG, Changan, BYD, Chery, BAIC, and Hongqi with bilingual English-Mandarin support and brand-approved repair garages.

Q: Can I insure my vintage or classic car?
A: Yes, we offer certified Classic Car Insurance with agreed-value policies and tailored coverage suited for collectibles and limited-use vehicles approved by RTA.

Q: What documents do I need to renew my car insurance?
A: You only need your Emirates ID (front & back), valid UAE Driving License, and your vehicle registration card (Mulkiya). Upload them using our AI OCR document scanner for instant auto-filling.

Q: Can I transfer my No Claim Discount (NCD) when I switch insurers?
A: Yes, eSanad automatically retrieves and transfers your NCD (up to 50% discount for 5 claim-free years) to your new policy.

Q: What is the difference between agency and non-agency repairs?
A: Agency repair ensures your vehicle is repaired directly at the official manufacturer dealership with original parts. Non-agency repair is conducted at certified RTA-approved local garages and is highly cost-effective.

HEALTH INSURANCE FAQ:

Q: Is health insurance mandatory in Dubai and Abu Dhabi?
A: Yes. Dubai Health Authority (DHA) and Department of Health - Abu Dhabi (DoH) regulations mandate health insurance for all citizens, expatriate residents, domestic workers, and visa dependents.

Q: What is an Essential Benefit Plan (EBP)?
A: An EBP is a low-cost, government-mandated health insurance plan for low-income workers (earning below AED 4,000/month) starting from AED 4/day, ensuring access to baseline emergency and medical care.

Q: Do you offer health insurance for Golden Visa holders and parents?
A: Yes, eSanad offers specialized DHA/DoH compliant Golden Visa health insurance plans starting from AED 4/day, as well as dedicated Parents Insurance plans for sponsored family members.

Q: What does cashless health insurance mean?
A: Cashless means when you seek treatment at any clinic or hospital within your plan's approved network (over 1,000+ in the UAE), you do not pay the bill. The hospital bills the insurer directly, and you only pay your designated co-payment (e.g. 10% or 20%).
