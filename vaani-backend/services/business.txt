MOTOR INSURANCE CLAIMS:

Step-by-Step Process:

1. At the Accident Scene:
   - Ensure safety first
   - Call police (999) if required (mandatory for accidents with injuries or major damage)
   - Don't move vehicles until police arrive (if required)
   - Take photos of damage and accident scene
   - Exchange information with other parties
   - Note witness details if any

2. Get Police Report:
   - Visit nearest police station
   - Provide license, registration, and insurance details
   - Obtain accident report within 24 hours
   - Report must indicate fault percentage

3. Notify Insurance Company:
   - Report claim within 24-48 hours
   - Through Esanad app or directly to insurer
   - Provide accident details and police report number

4. Submit Documents:
   Required Documents:
   - Police report
   - Driving license copy
   - Vehicle registration card
   - Insurance policy copy
   - Photos of damage
   - Repair quotation (if requested)

5. Vehicle Inspection:
   - Insurance surveyor inspects damage
   - Repair approval given
   - Choose approved garage

6. Repair Authorization:
   - Get repair approval from insurance
   - Select from approved garages list
   - Agency repair (if covered) or local garage

7. Collect Vehicle:
   - Pay deductible (if applicable)
   - Collect repaired vehicle
   - Obtain repair invoice and warranty

Claim Settlement Time:
- Simple claims: 7-14 days
- Complex claims: 14-30 days
- Total loss claims: 30-45 days

HEALTH INSURANCE CLAIMS:

Two Types of Claims:

A. Cashless Claims:
   - Use health card at network hospital
   - Treatment pre-authorized by insurance
   - No upfront payment (except co-pay)
   - Direct billing to insurance company

B. Reimbursement Claims:
   - Pay hospital directly
   - Submit claim to insurance
   - Get reimbursed as per policy terms
   
   Required Documents:
   - Original invoices and receipts
   - Medical reports and prescriptions
   - Diagnostic test results
   - Discharge summary (if hospitalized)
   - Pre-authorization (if applicable)
   
   Processing Time: 14-30 days

TRAVEL INSURANCE CLAIMS:

Process:
1. Contact emergency assistance number (24/7)
2. Follow their guidance for medical treatment
3. Keep all receipts and documentation
4. Submit claim form with supporting documents
5. Receive reimbursement as per policy

Required Documents:
- Claim form
- Original receipts
- Medical reports (if medical claim)
- Police report (if theft/loss)
- Airline/hotel confirmation (if delay claim)
- Boarding passes

Processing Time: 21-45 days

HOME INSURANCE CLAIMS:

Process:
1. Secure property and prevent further damage
2. Notify police (for theft/burglary)
3. Contact insurance company immediately
4. Document damage with photos/videos
5. Get repair quotes (if instructed)
6. Submit claim with all documents

Required Documents:
- Police report (if theft)
- Photos of damage
- Proof of ownership for valuable items
- Repair quotations
- Receipts for emergency repairs

Processing Time: 14-30 days

SECTION 12: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================

GENERAL QUESTIONS:

Q: Is Esanad an insurance company?
A: No, Esanad is an insurance comparison platform and broker. We partner with 17+ licensed insurance companies in UAE to provide you with multiple quotes and help you choose the best policy.

Q: Do I pay extra for using Esanad?
A: No, the prices you see on Esanad are the same as if you bought directly from the insurance company. There are no additional fees.

Q: How quickly can I get my insurance policy?
A: For motor and travel insurance, you can get your policy instantly (within minutes) after completing payment and document upload. Health insurance may take 24-48 hours for approval.

Q: Is my payment information secure?
A: Yes, all payments are processed through PCI DSS compliant secure payment gateways with 256-bit SSL encryption.

Q: Can I cancel my policy?
A: Yes, you can cancel within the 14-day cooling-off period for a full refund (if no claims made). Mid-term cancellations are subject to terms and conditions.

MOTOR INSURANCE FAQ:

Q: Is motor insurance mandatory in UAE?
A: Yes, at least third-party motor insurance is mandatory by UAE law for all vehicles.

Q: What is no-claim bonus?
A: No-claim bonus (NCB) is a discount you earn for every claim-free year, up to 50% after 5 years. It reduces your premium on renewal.

Q: Can I transfer my NCB from another insurance company?
A: Yes, you can transfer your NCB. You'll need to provide your previous policy showing claim-free years.

Q: What if I have an accident?
A: Contact police immediately (if required), get a police report, notify your insurance company within 24-48 hours, and follow the claims process.

Q: Can I drive my car outside UAE?
A: Only if you have GCC coverage extension. Otherwise, your insurance is valid only in UAE.

Q: What is the difference between agency repair and local garage repair?
A: Agency repair means your car will be repaired at the authorized brand dealership with original parts. Local garage repair uses aftermarket parts and is usually less expensive but not always original.

Q: How long does it take to get my car repaired?
A: Typically 5-15 days depending on damage severity and parts availability.

Q: Do I need to pay anything when making a claim?
A: You may need to pay the deductible amount if specified in your policy (e.g., AED 500, AED 1,000).

HEALTH INSURANCE FAQ:

Q: Is health insurance mandatory in UAE?
A: Yes, it's mandatory in Dubai and Abu Dhabi. Other emirates are progressively implementing mandatory health insurance.

Q: Can I choose any hospital?
A: You can only use hospitals and clinics in your insurance network for cashless treatment. Out-of-network treatment may require reimbursement claims with limits.

Q: What is co-payment?
A: Co-payment is the percentage of treatment cost you pay from your pocket. For example, 20% co-pay means insurance pays 80% and you pay 20%.

Q: Are pre-existing conditions covered?
A: Pre-existing conditions typically have a waiting period of 6-12 months before they're covered.

Q: Does health insurance cover COVID-19 treatment?
A: Most modern health insurance policies cover COVID-19 treatment. Check your specific policy wording.

Q: Can I add my family members to my policy?
A: Yes, most policies allow you to add spouse and children to create a family floater plan.

Q: What happens if I need treatment outside UAE?
A: Standard UAE health insurance typically doesn't cover treatment outside UAE unless you have international coverage or emergency evacuation coverage.

TRAVEL INSURANCE FAQ:

Q: When should I buy travel insurance?
A: Ideally, buy it as soon as you book your trip to get maximum coverage including trip cancellation.

Q: Does travel insurance cover COVID-19?
A: Many policies now include COVID-19 coverage. Check the specific policy details when purchasing.

Q: What if I need to cancel my trip?
A: If you cancel for a covered reason (illness, family emergency, etc.), travel insurance can reimburse non-refundable costs.

Q: Are adventure sports covered?
A: Standard policies usually don't cover adventure sports. You need to purchase additional coverage for activities like skiing, scuba diving, etc.

Q: What is the difference between single trip and annual multi-trip?
A: Single trip covers one journey. Annual multi-trip covers unlimited trips within a year (usually each trip up to 30 or 90 days).

HOME INSURANCE FAQ:

Q: Do I need home insurance as a tenant?
A: While not mandatory, it's highly recommended to protect your contents and personal belongings.

Q: What's the difference between building insurance and contents insurance?
A: Building insurance covers the structure (walls, roof, etc.) - usually landlord's responsibility. Contents insurance covers your personal belongings - tenant's responsibility.

Q: Are domestic helpers covered?
A: Some home insurance policies include domestic helper coverage. Check policy details or add as optional cover.

Q: What if I'm away for an extended period?
A: Inform your insurance company. Some policies have limits on unoccupied property (e.g., 30-60 days). Extended absence may affect coverage.

CLAIMS FAQ:

Q: How do I report a claim?
A: You can report claims through the Esanad mobile app, website, or by contacting customer service. We'll guide you through the process.

Q: How long does claim settlement take?
A: Motor insurance: 7-30 days, Health insurance: 14-30 days (reimbursement), Travel insurance: 21-45 days, Home insurance: 14-30 days.

Q: Can Esanad help with my claim?
A: Yes, Esanad provides claims assistance and acts as a liaison between you and the insurance company.

Q: What if my claim is rejected?
A: You can appeal the decision. Esanad can help you understand the reason and assist with the appeal process if justified.

Q: Will my premium increase after a claim?
A: Motor insurance premiums may increase and you may lose your no-claim bonus. Health and other insurance types generally don't increase premiums for claims.
